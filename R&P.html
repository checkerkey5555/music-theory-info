             RHYTHM & PERCUSSION
						 
             Rhythm and percussion can be a little confusing at least for me but certain genres have certain cliches
             and techniques that go into the rhythm and percussion so let's talk about it 
						 
             also little disclaimer i will be using the american way of talking about rhythm and not using english
             terms like crotchets or quavers because A the american system is so much better and b the american 
             system is just the german system translated to english
						 
             -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
             KICK SNARE PATTERN
						 
             here by putting a bar into eighth notes you can see the kick snare pattern this pattern is so common 
             ubiquitos in western music it defines alot of synthwave and is the basis more alot of drum groves
						 
             |RHYTHM:  4/4|1 e & a 2 e & a 3 e & a 4 e & a ||
             |INSTRU:SNARE|. . . . X . . . . . . . X . . . ||
             |INSTRU: KICK|X . . . . . . . X . . . . . . . ||
						 
             ROCK KICK SNARE PATTERN

             this is kind of another version of this same thing but the kick placed on the sixth beat is played
             on the 6th instead
						
             |RHYTHM:  4/4|1 e & a 2 e & a 3 e & a 4 e & a ||
             |INSTRU:SNARE|. . . . X . . . . . . . X . . . ||

             3/4 OVER 4/4 DRUM BEAT
						 
             this drum pattern shows you that someetimes by just playing a diffrent beat in a different time
						 signnature over 4/4 even for a bar can create intresting grooves and also that adding delay
             to certian things can also create this effect
						 
             |RHYTHM:  4/4|1 e & a 2 e & a 3 e & a 4 e & a ||
             |INSTRU:  N/A|X . . X . . X . . X . . X . . X ||
             |INSTRU:SNARE|. . . . X . . . . . . . X . . . ||
             |INSTRU: KICK|X . . . . . . . X . . . . . . . ||
