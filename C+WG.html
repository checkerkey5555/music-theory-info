             CHORDTONES + WHOLETONE GUIDE
             so the jist is that this is a rule of thumb method to find a good scale that you can use to improvise under
             certain chords or make melodies and other things                                

             what you do is get the chordtones of the chord and add on wholetone to the right of every note until all 
             and that creates a good scale you can play over or make a melody

             CHEAT SHEET FOR DIFFRENT CHORD TYPES                                             
             this is a sheet for what scales you should play over certain chords all the first ones come from the chordtones + 
             wholetone method and some from experince in playing and just what i think sounds good

             Major7/Major69/Major6 = Lydian Scale/Major Scale/Major Blues                                  

             Minor7th = Dorain Scale/Minor Scale/Minor Blues                                      

             Dominant7th = Mixolydian Scale/Lydian Dominant (also known as the acoustic scale)/Minor Blues                              

             Dominant7b5/Augmented7th = WholeTone Scale      
						 
             Augmented7th = Wholetone Scale + the 7th

             MinorMajor7th = Melodic Minor Scale/Harmonic Minor Scale
						 
             Diminished = Diminished Pentatonic Scale

             Diminished7th = Diminshed Scale                                                           

             Halfdiminished7th = Half Diminshed Scale 
						 
             Sus2 = Major Pentatonic
						 
             Sus4 = Yo Scale
